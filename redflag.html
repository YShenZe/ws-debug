Introduction

The Communist Manifesto, written by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, was first published in 1848. This influential document outlined the principles of Communism, analyzing the struggles between the working class (proletariat) and the ruling class (bourgeoisie). The manifesto called for the abolition of class structures and advocated for the liberation of the working class worldwide.

Key Principles

1. Historical Materialism: Marx and Engels argued that history is driven by material conditions and economic forces, rather than ideas or ideals. The conflict between different social classes defines the course of history.

2. Class Struggle: The manifesto highlights the conflict between the proletariat and the bourgeoisie as the central struggle in capitalist society. It calls for the proletariat to rise and overthrow the ruling class.

3. Revolution and Abolition of Private Property: The manifesto advocates for a revolution that would abolish private property, particularly the means of production, to establish a classless society.

Impact and Legacy

The Communist Manifesto has had a profound impact on the political landscape worldwide. It inspired numerous revolutions, the formation of socialist and communist parties, and the establishment of socialist states. The ideas within the manifesto have sparked ongoing debates about capitalism, class, and economic systems.
